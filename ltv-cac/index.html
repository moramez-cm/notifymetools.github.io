The number that decides it: LTV : CAC

Your CAC is what it truly costs to win one customer. Your LTV is what that customer is worth over all their orders. The ratio between them tells you if growth makes you richer or poorer. A widely-used benchmark: 3:1 is healthy, under 1:1 you lose money on every customer, and above 5:1 you may be under-investing in growth.

How to improve your ratio

    How to read this: the math is exact on the numbers you entered, but the inputs are estimates, especially lifetime orders per customer, which is the single biggest lever and the hardest to know. Pull it from your real repeat-purchase data if you can. Treat this as a directional check on whether your unit economics work, not an accounting statement. The 3:1 benchmark is a widely-cited rule of thumb, not a universal law; capital-efficient or fast-growth businesses sometimes run differently on purpose.
