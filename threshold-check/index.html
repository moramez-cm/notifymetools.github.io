A new card-network rule counts your refunds and chargebacks together. Cross the line and your payment processor is required to investigate you. Check your store in 30 seconds.

What's this about, in plain English

From July 24, 2026, Mastercard requires your payment processor to investigate any store whose refunds + chargebacks combined go over 5% of orders in a rolling 30-day window (once you've done 500+ orders). The catch: it counts normal refunds too, not just chargebacks, so a healthy store with lots of returns can get caught.

Visa has a separate, stricter rule: fraud + chargebacks over 1.5% (live since April 2026). This tool checks your numbers against both.
