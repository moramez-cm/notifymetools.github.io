Sales look fine, but the money never seems to be there. That's because margin leaks in five predictable places, and most merchants never look at all five together. Answer a few questions and see which leak is most likely draining your store.

The five places profit hides

Every order quietly loses margin to some mix of: payment & platform fees, shipping you absorb, discounts, returns & refunds, and wasted ad spend. Each feels small per order. Together they're often the gap between "we're growing" and "where did the money go." This ranks which is most likely hurting you.

1. Do you know your true take-home per order, after every fee?
Payment processing, transaction fees, app fees, the lot.
2. How do you handle shipping?
Absorbing shipping eats margin fast if your AOV is low.
3. How often do you run discounts / promos?
Frequent discounting trains customers to never pay full price.
4. What's your return / refund rate like?
Returns cost you the refund plus shipping, restocking, and lost margin.
5. How much of your sales rely on paid ads?
Rising ad costs can quietly push you to losing money per new customer.
6. Do repeat customers make up much of your revenue?
Low repeat rate means you keep paying to acquire, amplifying ad leakage.

The five-leak framework reflects well-established ecommerce unit-economics principles and 2026 reporting on Shopify margins: merchants commonly understate true CAC by counting only ad spend; absorbing shipping at low AOV erodes margin; frequent discounting compresses effective margin (a 20% discount at 40% margin needs ~100% more units to break even); returns carry costs well beyond the refund; and low repeat-purchase rates force repeated acquisition spend.
